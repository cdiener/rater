Evaluation criteria

A quick reminder...


Abstract

The points are given for the better of the two abstracts.

The abstract is evaluated by series of binary requirements. The final punctuation is the number of met requirements. For instance if the abstract meets 3 requirements it gets 3 points.

Requirements:

  • Relevance: The abstract is relevant to Systems Biology or Systems Medicine.
  • Structure: The abstract is well-structured and clearly defines the problem, the applied solution and its importance.
  • Results/completeness: The project provides significant scientific results (in contrast to only providing a new method or a perspective) and therefore appears complete.
  • Creativity and Novelty: The stated problem was solved in a creative and novel manner which could not have been achieved by standard or trivial methods.

English language profiency

0 points are awarded for applicants that do not seem to speak english (for instance if they fill out the registration in spanish). 1 point is awarded for average proficiency (can probably communicate in English but might not be completely fluent). 2 points are awarded for apparently fluent english (either determined by the abstract or a detailed Research Topic description). Short talks require 2 points and posters 1 point.
